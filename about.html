Family Background

  • Father: Hermann Einstein (Engineer)
  • Mother: Pauline Koch (Musician)
  • Spouses: Mileva Marić, Elsa Einstein
  • Children: Hans Albert, Eduard Einstein

Albert Einstein was born into a middle-class Jewish family. His father and uncle owned an electrical engineering company, which played a significant role in shaping Einstein’s interest in science.

Music was also an important part of his life. Einstein loved playing the violin and believed music helped him think creatively.

Early Life & Biography

Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany. From an early age, he showed deep curiosity about nature, mathematics, and the laws governing the universe.

He was often misunderstood by teachers because he questioned traditional teaching methods. Einstein preferred self-learning and independent thinking over memorization.

After completing his education in Switzerland, he struggled to find academic work and took a job at the Swiss Patent Office. This period later became one of the most productive times of his life.

Scientific Thinking & Philosophy

Einstein believed imagination was more powerful than knowledge. He often stated that curiosity, creativity, and critical thinking were essential to understanding the universe.

He rejected blind authority and encouraged questioning everything. This mindset helped him challenge existing scientific beliefs and introduce revolutionary ideas.

The Miracle Year (1905)

In 1905, Einstein published four groundbreaking scientific papers. These papers explained the photoelectric effect, Brownian motion, special relativity, and mass-energy equivalence.

These discoveries changed physics forever and made Einstein one of the most respected scientists in history.

Later Life

In the 1930s, due to the rise of Nazism in Germany, Einstein moved to the United States. He accepted a position at Princeton University, where he spent the rest of his career.

He continued researching unified field theory and actively participated in discussions on philosophy, education, and world peace.

Humanitarian Views

Albert Einstein was a strong supporter of peace and human rights. He spoke against war, racism, and nuclear weapons.

He believed science should serve humanity and help create a better and fairer world.

Death & Legacy

Albert Einstein passed away on April 18, 1955, in Princeton, USA. Even after his death, his ideas continue to influence modern science and technology.

Today, Einstein is remembered not only as a scientist but as a symbol of intelligence, creativity, and peace.
