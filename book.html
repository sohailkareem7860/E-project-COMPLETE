Books & Publications

A comprehensive list of Albert Einstein’s books, research papers, and publications that shaped modern physics.

Zur Elektrodynamik bewegter Körper

1905 • Research Paper

Einstein’s groundbreaking paper on special relativity, introducing the idea that space and time are not absolute. This paper revolutionized our understanding of the universe and laid the foundation for modern physics.

Learn More

Does the Inertia of a Body Depend Upon Its Energy Content?

1905 • Research Paper

This paper introduced the famous equation E = mc², establishing the equivalence of mass and energy. It has had profound implications in nuclear physics and energy studies.

Learn More

The Meaning of Relativity

1922 • Book

Einstein’s own explanation of his theories of special and general relativity, written for a broader audience. This work clarifies complex concepts with detailed mathematical support and historical context.

Learn More

Relativity: The Special and General Theory

1916 • Book

A non-technical introduction to both theories of relativity, aimed at general readers who are curious about the physics of the universe. Einstein explains his work in a step-by-step manner while providing philosophical insights into space and time.

Learn More

Ideas and Opinions

1954 • Collection

A posthumous collection of Einstein’s essays, letters, and reflections on science, politics, philosophy, and humanism. This book offers a deep understanding of his intellectual and moral worldview.

Learn More

Einstein’s Papers on the Quantum Theory of Radiation

1905–1925 • Research Papers

A collection of Einstein’s research on quantum theory, including explanations of the photoelectric effect, stimulated emission, and early contributions to quantum mechanics. These papers were instrumental in shaping modern quantum physics.

Learn More

On the Electrodynamics of Moving Bodies

1905 • Paper

Often cited as the most important physics paper of the 20th century, it introduces special relativity and redefines how we understand time, space, and motion.

Learn More

Autobiographical Notes

1949 • Collection

Einstein reflects on his life, scientific thinking, and the development of his theories. Offers rare insight into his thought processes and the challenges he faced in both science and society.

Learn More

For beginners exploring Einstein’s work, start with Relativity: The Special and General Theory for accessible explanations. For deeper scientific insight, The Meaning of Relativity and his collected research papers are essential. Ideas and Opinions offers a broader perspective on his philosophy and humanitarian thoughts.
